Data Analysis Project

Customer Data Analysis & Segmentation

A comprehensive analysis of 2,000 customer records to uncover spending patterns, demographic insights, and actionable business recommendations through data cleaning, exploration, and visualization.

Python Pandas NumPy Matplotlib Data Cleaning Statistical Analysis

Executive Dashboard - Key metrics and insights at a glance

1

Understanding the Data

The project began with a raw dataset of 2,000 customer records containing demographic information, income levels, and spending behavior scores. The goal was to transform this data into actionable insights for business stakeholders.

Dataset Features

  • CustomerID - Unique identifier for each customer
  • Gender - Male/Female classification
  • Age - Customer age (0-99 years)
  • Annual Income ($) - Yearly earnings
  • Spending Score (1-100) - Behavior-based spending metric
  • Profession - Customer's occupation
  • Work Experience - Years in workforce
  • Family Size - Number of family members
2

Data Cleaning & Preparation

Raw data is rarely perfect. Before any analysis could begin, I performed thorough data cleaning to ensure accuracy and reliability of results.

Cleaning Steps Performed

  • Duplicate Check: Verified no duplicate records existed
  • Missing Values: Found 35 missing profession values - filled with mode (Artist)
  • Invalid Data: Removed 2 records with spending scores outside valid range (1-100)
  • Text Standardization: Normalized gender and profession formatting
  • Outlier Detection: Identified 5 work experience outliers using IQR method
  • Data Type Optimization: Converted categorical columns for memory efficiency

Pattern Discovery & Correlation Analysis

One of the most important discoveries came from analyzing the relationships between variables. The correlation analysis revealed a counterintuitive finding.

Correlation Heatmap

Relationships between all numeric variables in the dataset.

Spending Behavior

Analysis of spending scores across demographics and professions.

Critical Finding: Income Does NOT Predict Spending
The correlation between Annual Income and Spending Score is only 0.02 - essentially no relationship. This challenges the common assumption that high earners are high spenders. Business strategies targeting only high-income customers may be missing significant opportunities.

Key Insights & Recommendations

The analysis revealed several actionable insights that can directly impact business strategy and marketing efforts.

🎯
Target Younger Demographics
Customers aged 18-25 have the highest spending score (53.6) compared to other age groups. Focus marketing on this demographic for higher conversion.
💰
Untapped High-Income Segment
31.4% of customers have high income but low spending. Targeted campaigns emphasizing value and quality could unlock this significant revenue potential.
🎨
Artists Are Top Spenders
34% of high-value customers are Artists. Consider profession-targeted marketing campaigns and partnerships in creative industries.
👥
Gender Balance in Marketing
Female customers show slightly higher spending (51.1 avg) and make up 59% of the customer base. Ensure marketing appeals to this demographic.
