01. The Origin

Histacruise started from a personal frustration. After several cruises, I wanted a proper way to track where I'd been — not just a spreadsheet, but something that could show me a map of every port I'd visited, how much I'd spent per cruise line, which ships I'd sailed, and how my trips compared over the years.

Every tool I found was either too basic or buried inside a cruise line's own app. So I built my own. What started as a simple history log grew into a full platform with live industry data, analytics dashboards, and a social community for sharing voyages with other cruisers.

The goal: Build the cruise tracker I actually wanted to use — one that treats personal travel data seriously and turns it into something visually meaningful.

02. The Problem

Cruise enthusiasts have no dedicated tool that combines personal history tracking, real-time industry data, and a social layer in one place. Existing options are spreadsheets, note apps, or scattered across multiple cruise line portals.

  • No visual history — no map showing where you've actually been across all cruise lines
  • No consolidated analytics — spending, nights at sea, cabin types, regions all scattered
  • No live context — no connection to what's happening in the industry (stock prices, news, deals)
  • No community — no way to share trips, follow other cruisers, or discover what others are experiencing

03. Key Features

🗺️

Interactive Port Map

Leaflet.js map with markers for every port visited. Each marker shows visit dates, cruise details, and links back to the full history entry.

📊

Spending Analytics

Chart.js dashboards breaking down spending by cruise line, region, cabin type, and year. Track total days at sea, favorite ships, and more.

📡

Live Data Pipeline

HC_Pipeline runs on a schedule — pulling cruise stock prices (CCL, RCL, NCLH), industry news, deals, and ship specs automatically.

👥

Social Community

Follow other cruisers, share posts about your voyages, react and comment, earn badges, and discover new users on the Discover page.

🔐

User Authentication

Flask-Login with secure session management. Registration auto-logs users in. Profiles are private by default with selective sharing.

🏆

Badges & Milestones

Achievement system that awards badges for cruise milestones — first voyage, ship hopper, globe trotter, days at sea thresholds, and more.

📸

Photo Galleries

Upload cruise photos and profile/cover images stored directly in the database as BLOBs — no external storage service required.

🔔

Notifications

Real-time notification system for follows, likes, and comments. Unread badge count updates on every page load.

04. The HC_Pipeline

One of the most technically interesting parts of Histacruise is the HC_Pipeline — a standalone data collection system that runs as a background service alongside the Flask app.

Rather than showing static data, Histacruise surfaces real, current information about the cruise industry every time you load the homepage. The pipeline is orchestrated by APScheduler and runs four collectors on different intervals:

  • Stock Collector: Pulls CCL, RCL, and NCLH share prices via yfinance. Shows current price, daily change, and sparklines on the dashboard.
  • News Collector: Parses RSS feeds from CruiseHive and other maritime publications. Stores headlines with links for the news section.
  • Deals Collector: Scrapes active promotions from major cruise lines. Falls back to generated sample deals when pages are inaccessible.
  • Ship Collector: Gathers technical specs for ships in the database. Attempts Wikipedia API; gracefully handles rate limiting.

Design decision: The pipeline runs in a daemon thread at startup so data is always ready on first visit — employers and users never see empty sections.
