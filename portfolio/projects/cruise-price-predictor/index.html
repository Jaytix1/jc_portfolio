Machine Learning Project

Cruise Price Predictor

A machine learning model that predicts cruise vacation prices based on destination, cruise line, cabin type, duration, and booking timing. Trained on 5,000 cruise records with 93.5% accuracy.

93.5% Prediction Accuracy (R2 Score: 0.935)
Python Scikit-learn Pandas Gradient Boosting Feature Engineering Data Visualization
1

The Problem

Cruise pricing is notoriously complex and opaque. Prices vary dramatically based on dozens of factors: the cruise line, destination, time of year, cabin type, how far in advance you book, and even the age of the ship. For travelers, this makes it nearly impossible to know if they're getting a good deal.

The goal: Build a machine learning model that can accurately predict cruise prices, helping travelers understand what drives costs and find the best value for their budget.

Key Questions to Answer

  • What factors most influence cruise pricing?
  • When is the best time to book for the lowest prices?
  • Which cruise lines offer the best value?
  • How much does cabin type really affect price?
2

Data & Feature Engineering

The model was trained on 5,000 cruise records with realistic pricing patterns based on industry data. Each record includes 9 features that capture the key factors affecting cruise prices.

Features Used

  • Cruise Line: 8 major lines (Carnival, Royal Caribbean, Disney, etc.)
  • Destination: 8 regions (Caribbean, Alaska, Mediterranean, etc.)
  • Cabin Type: Interior, Ocean View, Balcony, Suite
  • Duration: Number of nights (3-14)
  • Departure Port: 10 major US ports
  • Departure Month: Captures seasonal pricing
  • Days Until Departure: Booking window timing
  • Ship Age: Newer ships command premium prices
  • Passengers: Number of travelers (1-4)

The data was preprocessed using StandardScaler for numerical features and OneHotEncoder for categorical variables, creating a robust feature set for model training.

Feature Importance Analysis

2 What Drives Cruise Prices?

Understanding which factors matter most in pricing

Click to enlarge

Reading the Chart

This horizontal bar chart shows the relative importance of each feature in predicting cruise prices. Longer bars indicate features that have more influence on the final prediction.

Duration (40% importance) is by far the most important factor. This makes intuitive sense - a 14-night cruise costs significantly more than a 4-night cruise regardless of other factors.

Suite Cabin (19%) ranks second, showing that cabin upgrades have a dramatic impact on price. Suites can cost 2-3x more than interior cabins.

Passengers (15%) comes third - more travelers means higher total cost, though there are economies of scale (third and fourth passengers often pay less per person).

Key Insight

Disney Cruise Line appears as a standalone feature at 5% importance, showing it commands premium pricing regardless of other factors - the "Disney tax" is real!

Price Factors Deep Dive

3 How Each Factor Affects Price

Detailed breakdown of pricing patterns

Click to enlarge

Cabin Type Impact

The top-left chart shows a clear pricing tier structure. Interior cabins average $20,036, while Suites jump to $48,189 - a 140% increase. Ocean View and Balcony fall in between at $25,045 and $29,853 respectively.

Tip: Ocean View offers only a 25% premium over Interior but adds natural light and a window - often the best value upgrade.

Cruise Line Pricing

Disney commands the highest average prices, followed by Celebrity and Holland America. Carnival and MSC offer the most budget-friendly options.

Note: Price doesn't always equal quality - Carnival's lower prices reflect their target market (families, first-timers) rather than inferior service.

Duration Effect

The bottom-left chart shows an almost linear relationship between cruise length and price. A 3-night cruise averages ~$8,000 while a 14-night cruise exceeds $60,000. However, the per-night cost actually decreases slightly on longer cruises.

Booking Window

Surprisingly, last-minute bookings (under 2 weeks) offer the lowest average prices! Booking 3-6 months out is actually the most expensive window.

Strategy: Book very early (6+ months) for selection, or wait for last-minute deals if you're flexible.

Actionable Insights for Travelers

  • Choose shorter cruises (3-5 nights) for budget trips - dramatically lower total cost
  • Ocean View cabins offer the best value-for-money upgrade
  • Consider Carnival or MSC for 30-40% savings vs premium lines
  • Be flexible on dates - last-minute deals can save 15-20%
  • Avoid booking 3-6 months out unless you need specific cabin selection

Technical Implementation

Tech Stack

  • Python 3.13: Core programming language
  • Pandas & NumPy: Data manipulation and numerical computing
  • Scikit-learn: ML models, preprocessing pipelines, cross-validation
  • Matplotlib & Seaborn: Data visualization
  • Joblib: Model serialization for deployment

Project Structure

  • generate_dataset.py: Creates realistic cruise pricing data with industry-accurate patterns
  • train_model.py: Trains 5 ML models with cross-validation, generates visualizations
  • predict.py: Interactive CLI for making predictions with the trained model
  • cruise_price_model.joblib: Serialized Gradient Boosting model for deployment

Future Enhancements

  • Flask/FastAPI web interface for browser-based predictions
  • Integration with Histacruise for personalized price recommendations
  • Web scraping real cruise prices for continuous model retraining
  • Price alert system that notifies users when deals appear
  • Comparison tool showing predicted vs actual prices from cruise websites
